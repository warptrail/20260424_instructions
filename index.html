🧬 Family archive pipeline

Family Photo & Genealogy Project

A simple, step-by-step system for preserving scanned family photos, identifying faces, and connecting those people to the family tree.

🎯 The goal

We want to make sure the people in these photos are not lost to time.

1. PreserveKeep original scans safe and unchanged.
2. IdentifyUse face recognition to group and name people.
3. ConnectAttach people and photos to the family tree.

🧭 Big picture

Raw Photos → Working Copies → Face Recognition → Named People → Family Tree → Legacy Archive
Key idea: do not try to organize everything perfectly at the start. First, protect the originals. Then identify people. Then build the genealogy structure.

📁 Phase 1 — Create a safe folder structure

Start by putting the entire archive in one stable place, ideally on the computer and backed up to an external drive.

FamilyArchive/
  photos_raw/        original scans — do not edit
  photos_working/    copies used for tagging and organizing
  exports/           final curated outputs later
  notes/             people list, research notes, questions
  • Never edit or delete anything in photos_raw.
  • Do all work in photos_working.
  • Do not rename thousands of files at the beginning.

🧠 Phase 2 — Use a photo system to identify faces

Recommended tool: Immich. This is the “photo brain” of the project. It scans photos, detects faces, groups similar faces together, and lets you name people.

What you do in this phase

  • Import the working photo copies.
  • Let the system scan and group faces.
  • Name people when you recognize them.
  • Merge duplicate groups when the same person appears twice.
  • Ignore strangers, background faces, or uncertain faces until later.
This is the highest-leverage step. Once faces are named, the archive becomes searchable and much easier to connect to family history.

📝 Phase 3 — Keep a simple people list

While identifying faces, keep a plain list of people and clues. This does not need to be perfect.

NameNotesRelationships
Mary JohnsonGrandma; appears in several 1950s photosMother of Susan
Robert “Bob” SmithPossibly Uncle BobBrother of Mary?
Unknown Woman ASeen in wedding photosAsk Aunt Linda

A spreadsheet, Word document, notebook, or simple text file is fine. The point is to avoid losing clues.

🌳 Phase 4 — Use genealogy software for the family tree

Recommended tool: Heredis. This is the “family tree brain” of the project. It stores people, relationships, dates, places, sources, and attached media.

Why Heredis instead of Ancestris?

ToolBest forTradeoff
HeredisEasier family-tree building, friendlier interface, polished reportsCosts money
AncestrisFree, open-source, GEDCOM-focused genealogy workMore technical and less friendly
Recommendation: use Heredis for the family tree unless the free/open-source requirement is more important than ease of use.

🔗 Phase 5 — Link photos to people

Do not try to make one program do everything.

1
Immich
Finds faces, groups people, and lets you search photos visually.
2
Heredis
Stores the family tree, relationships, facts, sources, and selected photos.
3
People list
Captures uncertain names, clues, nicknames, and questions.

When a person is clearly identified, attach their best photos to their person record in Heredis. Keep Immich as the searchable photo archive.

💾 External hard drive answer

Yes, the data can live on an external hard drive.

  • Photos can be stored on the external drive.
  • Genealogy project files can be stored there too.
  • The programs themselves usually install on the computer.
Simple translation: the data is portable; the app is usually installed locally.

🔒 Phase 6 — Backup rule

If there is no backup, the project is not safe.

  • Keep one copy on the main computer.
  • Keep one copy on an external hard drive.
  • Ideally keep one more copy in cloud storage or on a second drive.

🚫 Avoid these traps

  • Do not start by manually sorting thousands of photos.
  • Do not rename everything before people are identified.
  • Do not edit the original scans.
  • Do not skip backups.
  • Do not expect genealogy software to do excellent face recognition.
